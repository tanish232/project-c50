Interesting Facts About Camel

  • Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes.
  • Camels have thick lips which let them forage for thorny plants other animals can't eat.
  • There are two types of camels: One humped or “dromedary” camels and two humped Bactrian camels.

Interesting Facts About Chameleon

  • they can move their eyes separately, with each eye having a field of 180 degrees.
  • 59 unique species of chameleon live on the island of Madagascar and are found nowhere else. This is over a quarter of the world’s 202 species.
  • Excluding their tail, some chameleons’ tongues can be twice the length of their body. Their tongues consist of both muscles and bones, and can bend round to grab their pray.

Interesting Facts About Panda

  • Giant pandas are good at climbing trees and can also swim..
  • Pandas are born looking like baby badgers — fur-less, pink, and blind. The iconic black and white colour comes later, after about three weeks.
  • A 45-kilo adult (and pandas can reach 150 kg in captivity) spends as long as 14 hours eating. And it can eat 12 to 38 kilos of bamboo a day.
